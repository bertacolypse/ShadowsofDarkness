Long ago....

In the depths of a forgotten temple, shrouded from the prying eyes of the world, two cloaked figures, Clerics of the Hidden Eye, gathered under the pale glow of a crescent moon. The temple, once revered for its grand altars and delicate frescoes, now lay in ruin, its pillars toppled, and walls whispering ancient secrets through deep cracks. Moss and ivy crept along the weathered stone, reclaiming the temple for nature.

Within the heart of the temple stood a vast chamber adorned with intricate carvings depicting elven deities of old. In the center of this chamber rested a well, its waters black as night, swirling with an eerie luminescence known as Black Icor. The well was surrounded by a circular array of glyphs, each symbol representing a fragment of forgotten knowledge.

Before them, a shadowy form emerged from the depths of the well. The figure solidified into Lahsatyr, the Shadow Demon, his dark and sinister form taking shape before their eyes. He towered over them, his silhouette flickering and warping like a living shadow cast by unseen hands.

Lahsatyr's presence distorted the very air around him, creating a chilling atmosphere that sent shivers down the spines of the clerics. His eyes burned like black coals, reflecting the sinister malevolence that coursed through his being. They seemed to pierce into the souls of those who dared stand before him.

His form seemed to blend seamlessly with the surrounding shadows, as though he was a creature born of darkness itself. Cloaked in an ebony shroud, his movements were unnaturally graceful, akin to the dance of a sinister phantom.

In the dim light of the crescent moon, his shadowy tendrils writhed with eerie fluidity, reaching out like extensions of his ominous aura. One of these dark tendrils shot forth from Lahsatyr's hand, wrapping around the throat of a male cleric with bone-chilling precision.

The cleric gasped for breath, his life force slowly being drained away. Lahsatyr's cruel smile widened as he effortlessly lifted the cleric into the air, the poor soul now suspended helplessly above the ground. The darkness in his eyes danced with delight at the cleric's suffering.

At the same time, another tendril of darkness slithered from Lahsatyr's form, winding its way toward a female cleric standing nearby. With a swift and sinister motion, the tendril coiled itself around the woman's wrist, drawing her closer to Lahsatyr's imposing presence.

Her eyes widened in terror as Lahsatyr's malevolence seeped into her very being, overpowering her will and replacing it with his own sinister intent. The once gentle and compassionate soul was now a vessel for Lahsatyr's malevolence, her features contorting with sinister glee.

Her haunting laughter echoed through the temple, mocking her former comrade. The possessed cleric's voice became a chilling melody of malevolence, and the other cleric could only watch in horror as her once trusted companion succumbed to Lahsatyr's dark influence.

The temple's sacred chamber, once a place of reverence, now bore witness to terror. The two clerics found themselves trapped in a nightmare of their own making, facing the malevolence they had unwittingly unleashed.

Behind Lahsatyr, the temple lay in ruins, its sacred halls now tainted with darkness. The world trembled under the weight of his malevolence, and the fate of Thordasha hung in the balance. No other survivors remained to bear witness to the horror that transpired within the hidden walls of the temple. The days ahead would be filled with darkness and uncertainty, and the fate of Thordasha would rest in the hands of those who dared to stand against the shadows.
